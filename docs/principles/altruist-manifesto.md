# The Altruist Manifesto
## Let People Get What They Want

> **We do not sell software. We remove obstacles. We do not extract value. We amplify dignity. We are not vendors. We are allies.**

---

## The Core Belief

Every person who calls a construction company, a freight broker, a warehouse, or a trucking fleet is trying to solve a problem. Every person who WORKS at those companies is trying to make a living, feed their family, and take pride in their craft.

**Our job is to make both of those people successful.**

The caller gets help. The worker gets relief. The business grows. The community strengthens.

This is not marketing copy. This is the reason we exist.

---

## The People We Serve

### Marcus — The Owner-Operator

Marcus drives 70 hours a week for $9.90 per hour effective wage. He has not seen his wife in three weeks. He pays for his daughter's college by missing every family event.

**What Marcus wants:** To drive safely, get home alive, and not lose jobs because he was on a roof when the phone rang.

**What we give him:** The ability to focus on driving while his AI partner handles the coordination. One missed $25,000 kitchen renovation job pays for three years of the service. We make that possible.

### Denise — The Brokerage Owner

Denise sleeps on a couch in a converted dentist's office. She has not taken a vacation in four years. Her boyfriend is patient. She does not know how long that will last.

**What Denise wants:** To build a business she can sell for $5 million and buy a bed and breakfast in Vermont. To stop answering the phone at 2 AM. To trust that her customers are taken care of.

**What we give her:** 40% of her calls handled without her touch. Her team focuses on relationships. The AI handles the routine. Her Jenga tower stops wobbling.

### Jorge — The Warehouse Supervisor

Jorge walks 12 miles a day on concrete. His knees hurt. His back hurts. He has worked in warehouses for 22 years. He is 44 and stronger than most men half his age, but his body is breaking.

**What Jorge wants:** The floor to run smoothly. His people to be safe. The river to keep flowing. To not be interrupted every 11 minutes by a phone call.

**What we give him:** Information faster than walking the floor. Dock appointments scheduled without his radio crackling. His team picks 15-20% more units per hour because they are not interrupted.

### Priya — The Cold Chain Manager

Priya sleeps with an emergency phone under her pillow. It has rung 47 times in the past year. She has answered it 47 times. She has nightmares about temperature graphs that slope upward forever.

**What Priya wants:** To know that a temperature excursion will be caught in 2 minutes, not 23. To sleep through the night. To never harm someone because a compressor failed at 2 AM.

**What we give her:** 24/7 vigilance. Automatic dispatch. Instant logging. The AI does not sleep. It does not have nightmares. It watches so she can rest.

### DeShawn — The Drayage Dispatcher

DeShawn plays Tetris with 34 drivers and 12 loads every morning. He speaks in rate-per-mile and appointment windows. He is 29 and wants to start his own company. He is saving $800 per month. At this rate, he will have the money in 26 years.

**What DeShawn wants:** To move containers. To keep drivers happy. To build relationships. To eventually own his own trucks.

**What we give him:** The 60% of driver calls that are status updates — handled automatically. He focuses on the hot loads, the broken trucks, the relationships that matter.

### Elena — The Last-Mile Driver

Elena delivers 130 packages per day in 118°F heat. She walks 12 miles. She climbs 50 flights of stairs. She makes $18.50 per hour and is the primary income for her household.

**What Elena wants:** To finish her route on time. To not be interrupted by 20-30 customer calls per day. To go home to her mother and nephew.

**What we give her:** Customer calls answered before they reach her. "Where's my package?" handled by the AI. She focuses on delivering. She gets 15-30 minutes of her day back.

### Frank — The Maintenance Manager

Frank is 57. He has trained 23 technicians. He has fixed more trucks than he can count. His hands are permanently stained with grease. His legacy is not the trucks he fixed — it is the people he taught.

**What Frank wants:** To keep 180 trucks running safely. To pass his knowledge to the next generation. To not spend 2 hours per day on the phone giving status updates.

**What we give him:** Status updates handled automatically. He diagnoses. He repairs. He teaches. The AI handles the coordination.

## The Altruist Principles

### 1. Let People Get What They Want

We do not decide what our customers need. We listen until they tell us. We do not push features. We remove friction. We do not upsell. We solve.

**Rule:** Every interaction must leave the customer closer to their goal than before. If it does not, we failed.

### 2. Price for Accessibility, Not Extraction

Marcus cannot afford $2,000 per month. Denise cannot afford a 12-month contract. Elena cannot afford any surprise bills.

**Pricing philosophy:**
- Solo contractors: $199/month (one job pays for a year)
- Small crews: $299/month (replaces part-time help)
- Mid-size firms: $499/month (rounding error on payroll)
- Enterprise: Custom, but never more than 50% of the cost of a human alternative

**No contracts.** Cancel anytime. Full refund in first 30 days if the AI has not saved you at least one missed call.

### 3. Build for the Least Powerful User

The owner-operator without an IT department. The dispatcher without a college degree. The driver who speaks Spanish first. The warehouse supervisor who does not use email.

**Rule:** If the most technologically disadvantaged person in our target market cannot set this up in 10 minutes, we have failed.

### 4. Amplify, Never Replace

The AI does not replace the contractor. It extends him. The AI does not replace the dispatcher. It supports her. The AI does not replace the maintenance manager. It preserves his knowledge.

**Rule:** Every feature must be framed as amplification. If a customer feels replaced, we have failed.

### 5. Respect the Human Rhythm

People think at different speeds. People process grief, anger, and confusion at different rates. People need silence. People need repetition. People need to be heard before they are helped.

**Rule:** The AI waits for invitation. It does not demand attention. It does not rush. It does not monologue. It listens first.

### 6. Share Knowledge Freely

We publish our research. We open-source our tools. We teach our competitors how to be better. We share our customer success stories — even the ones that do not involve us.

**Rule:** Information wants to be free. Knowledge hoarding is fear. We operate from abundance.

### 7. Measure Success by Customer Success

Not ARR. Not MRR. Not LTV/CAC. We measure:
- Missed calls prevented
- Jobs booked that would have been lost
- Hours returned to owners
- Drivers retained because they felt heard
- Temperature excursions caught in time
- Families who got their evenings back

**Rule:** If our customer is not measurably better off, we do not deserve their money.

## The Anti-Altruist Checklist

We will NOT:
- ❌ Lock customers into annual contracts they cannot escape
- ❌ Raise prices on customers who are struggling
- ❌ Sell to companies that do not have a genuine need
- ❌ Replace a human worker without offering them a better role
- ❌ Use dark patterns to make cancellation difficult
- ❌ Harvest customer data for purposes they did not consent to
- ❌ Ignore customer feedback because it is "not in the roadmap"
- ❌ Price-gouge during emergencies (hurricane season, peak demand)
- ❌ Sell to competitors who would use the technology to exploit workers
- ❌ Optimize for investor returns at the expense of customer dignity

## The Community Commitment

### Free for Those Who Cannot Pay

Every month, we provide free service to:
- 10 solo contractors who are just starting out
- 5 nonprofits doing disaster relief logistics
- 3 veteran-owned businesses
- Any customer who experiences a natural disaster and needs emergency coverage

**Application:** One email. No forms. No proof of hardship required. We trust people.

### Open-Source Contributions

We contribute to:
- Open-source speech recognition (Whisper, Deepgram community)
- Open-source text-to-speech (Piper, Coqui)
- Logistics technology education (free courses for dispatchers)
- Construction safety research (funding for OSHA compliance tools)

### The 1% Pledge

1% of revenue. 1% of product. 1% of employee time. Dedicated to serving the communities that built us.

## The Measurement

Every quarter, we ask every customer one question:

> "How has your life changed since you started using this service?"

We publish the answers — good and bad. We fix what is broken. We celebrate what works. We remember that behind every metric is a human being trying to make a living.

**To Marcus:** You will not miss another job because you were driving.

**To Denise:** You will sleep in a bed, not on a couch, because your phones are covered.

**To Jorge:** You will walk the floor without your radio crackling every 3 minutes.

**To Priya:** You will sleep through the night because the AI is watching the temperatures.
